# Stakeholder Interview and Meeting Guides

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose of the Document

- **Objective**: This document serves as a comprehensive guide for planning and
  conducting stakeholder interviews and meetings during the discovery phase of
  a project.
- **Goals**:
  - Ensure effective communication with stakeholders.
  - Gather essential information to fill knowledge gaps.
  - Build strong relationships with key stakeholders.

### 1.2 Scope

- **Coverage**:
  - Stakeholders within the client organization, including technical teams,
    managers, and executives.
  - Meetings and interviews conducted during the discovery phase.
- **Exclusions**:
  - Direct interactions with external vendors unless specified.

### 1.3 How to Use This Guide

- **Instructions**:
  - Utilize the guide to prepare for each stakeholder interaction.
  - Customize templates and questions to fit the specific context of each
    meeting.
  - Refer to best practices for effective communication and meeting
    facilitation.

## 2. Stakeholder Identification and Analysis

### 2.1 Overview of Stakeholders

- **Key Stakeholder Groups**:
  - **Technical Teams**: Responsible for specific technical aspects of the
    project.
  - **Management Teams**: Oversee project execution and resource allocation.
  - **Project Sponsors**: Provide strategic direction and approve project
    deliverables.
  - **End Users/Early Adopters**: Utilize the project's outcomes and provide
    feedback.

### 2.2 Stakeholder Profiles

- **Template for Each Stakeholder**:
  - **Name/Team**: [Stakeholder Name or Team]
  - **Role and Responsibilities**: Describe their position and duties within
    the organization.
  - **Relevance to Project**: Explain how their work relates to the project.
  - **Influence and Interest Level**: Assess their potential impact on the
    project.
  - **Communication Preferences**: Preferred methods and times for
    communication.
  - **Key Concerns and Interests**: Any known objectives or reservations they
    may have.

_Repeat this template for each identified stakeholder or team._

## 3. Meeting Preparation

### 3.1 Setting Meeting Objectives

- **Guidelines**:
  - Clearly define what you aim to achieve in each meeting.
  - Align objectives with the overall goals of the discovery phase.
  - **Examples**:
    - Gather detailed information on current workflows.
    - Understand specific requirements and constraints.

### 3.2 Developing Discussion Topics

- **Strategies**:
  - Identify topics relevant to each stakeholder's expertise.
  - Prioritize topics based on importance and information gaps.
  - Ensure topics are aligned with meeting objectives.

### 3.3 Crafting Effective Questions

#### 3.3.1 Open-Ended Questions

- **Importance**: Encourage detailed responses and insights.
- **Tips**:
  - Use "how," "what," "why" questions.
  - Avoid questions that can be answered with a simple "yes" or "no."
- **Examples**:
  - "What challenges do you face with the current systems?"
  - "How do you envision the project improving your team's workflows?"

#### 3.3.2 Probing Questions

- **Purpose**: Dig deeper into responses to uncover underlying issues.
- **Techniques**:
  - Ask follow-up questions based on initial answers.
  - Seek clarification or examples.
- **Examples**:
  - "Can you elaborate on the challenges you mentioned?"
  - "What specific features would address the limitations you've experienced?"

### 3.4 Agenda Creation

#### 3.4.1 Agenda Template

- **Components**:
  - **Meeting Title**
  - **Date, Time, Location** (or Virtual Meeting Link)
  - **Attendees**
  - **Objectives**
  - **Agenda Items with allocated times**
  - **Any preparatory materials needed**

#### 3.4.2 Sample Agendas

- **Customize for Each Stakeholder**:
  - **Technical Team Meeting**:
    - Introduction (5 min)
    - Current State Analysis (15 min)
    - Challenges and Pain Points (15 min)
    - Desired Future State (10 min)
    - Next Steps and Action Items (5 min)
  - **Management Team Meeting**:
    - Introduction (5 min)
    - Project Overview (10 min)
    - Resource Allocation and Requirements (15 min)
    - Risk Assessment and Mitigation (15 min)
    - Next Steps and Action Items (5 min)

### 3.5 Logistics and Scheduling

- **Best Practices**:
  - Coordinate schedules considering participants' availability.
  - Send calendar invites with all necessary details.
  - Provide agendas and any pre-reading materials in advance.
  - Confirm meetings a day before.

## 4. Meeting Execution

### 4.1 Conducting the Meeting

#### 4.1.1 Opening the Meeting

- **Steps**:
  - Welcome attendees and thank them for their time.
  - Introduce yourself and any team members.
  - Clearly state the meeting objectives.
  - Outline the agenda.

#### 4.1.2 Facilitating the Discussion

- **Techniques**:
  - Encourage participation from all attendees.
  - Listen actively and show appreciation for their input.
  - Keep the discussion focused on agenda topics.
  - Monitor time to cover all items.

#### 4.1.3 Handling Challenges

- **Strategies**:
  - Address any resistance by acknowledging concerns.
  - Clarify misunderstandings promptly.
  - Remain patient and respectful, even if discussions become difficult.

### 4.2 Note-Taking and Documentation

#### 4.2.1 Note-Taking Template

- **Sections**:
  - **Meeting Details**: Date, time, participants.
  - **Key Discussion Points**: Summarized bullet points.
  - **Decisions Made**: Any agreements or conclusions.
  - **Action Items**: Tasks, responsible persons, due dates.
  - **Next Steps**: Follow-up actions or meetings.

#### 4.2.2 Best Practices for Note-Taking

- **Tips**:
  - Capture essential information without transcribing verbatim.
  - Use shorthand or symbols for efficiency.
  - Highlight or flag important points for emphasis.

### 4.3 Closing the Meeting

- **Actions**:
  - Summarize main takeaways and confirm understanding.
  - Review action items and assign responsibilities.
  - Discuss any required follow-up.
  - Thank participants for their contributions.

## 5. Post-Meeting Activities

### 5.1 Meeting Summary and Distribution

- **Guidelines**:
  - Prepare a concise meeting summary promptly after the meeting.
  - Include key points, decisions, and action items.
  - Distribute to attendees and any relevant stakeholders.

### 5.2 Action Item Tracking

#### 5.2.1 Action Items Log

- **Components**:
  - **Description of the Action Item**
  - **Assigned to** (responsible person/team)
  - **Due Date**
  - **Status Updates**

#### 5.2.2 Follow-Up Procedures

- **Strategies**:
  - Set reminders for upcoming deadlines.
  - Check in with responsible parties as needed.
  - Update the action items log regularly.

## 6. Interview and Meeting Questions Bank

### 6.1 General Questions

- **Examples**:
  - "Can you describe your team's current workflows related to this project?"
  - "What are your expectations from this initiative?"
  - "Are there any concerns or risks you foresee?"

### 6.2 Stakeholder-Specific Questions

#### 6.2.1 Technical Teams

- **Questions**:
  - "What is the current state of the technical infrastructure?"
  - "What limitations have you experienced with existing systems?"
  - "How does your team currently manage scalability and reliability?"

#### 6.2.2 Management Teams

- **Questions**:
  - "Can you provide an overview of the existing project management processes?"
  - "What challenges do you face with current workflows?"
  - "How is resource allocation currently managed?"

#### 6.2.3 Project Sponsors

- **Questions**:
  - "What are the primary business objectives driving this project?"
  - "How do you measure success for this initiative?"
  - "What are your expectations regarding timelines and deliverables?"

#### 6.2.4 End Users/Early Adopters

- **Questions**:
  - "What features are most critical for your adoption of this platform?"
  - "What support would you need during the transition?"
  - "How does your team currently integrate with other systems or platforms?"

## 8. Best Practices and Tips

### 8.1 Effective Communication

- **Recommendations**:
  - Use clear, jargon-free language to accommodate all audience levels.
  - Confirm understanding by paraphrasing key points.
  - Be attentive to verbal and non-verbal cues.

### 8.2 Cultural and Organizational Awareness

- **Insights**:
  - Familiarize yourself with the client's organizational structure.
  - Respect internal protocols and hierarchies.
  - Be aware of the company's culture regarding change and innovation.

### 8.3 Managing Time

- **Strategies**:
  - Start and end meetings on time.
  - Prioritize topics to ensure critical areas are covered.
  - Be flexible but guide the conversation back if it strays off-topic.

### 8.4 Building Rapport

- **Tips**:
  - Show genuine interest in stakeholders' perspectives.
  - Find common ground or shared goals.
  - Maintain professionalism while being approachable.

## 9. Challenges and Mitigation Strategies

### 9.1 Potential Challenges

- **Examples**:
  - **Resistance to Change**: Stakeholders may be hesitant about new systems or
    processes.
  - **Limited Availability**: Difficulty scheduling meetings with busy
    stakeholders.
  - **Incomplete Information**: Stakeholders may not have all the answers or
    necessary details.

### 9.2 Mitigation Strategies

- **Approaches**:
  - **Resistance to Change**: Emphasize benefits and address concerns directly.
  - **Limited Availability**: Offer flexible meeting times or asynchronous
    communication options.
  - **Incomplete Information**: Identify alternative sources or plan for
    follow-up discussions.
